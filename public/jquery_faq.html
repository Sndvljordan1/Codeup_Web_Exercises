Parks List

Arches

  • There are over 2,000 cataloged arches in Arches National Park. In order to be considered an arch, an opening must measure at least three feet (in any direction).
  • Arches is located in a "high desert," with elevations ranging from 4,085 to 5,653 feet above sea level.
  • On a daily basis, temperatures may fluctuate as much as 50 degrees.
  • The forces of nature have acted in concert to create the landscape of Arches, which contains the greatest density of natural arches in the world.
  • On average, the park receives 8-10 inches (18-23 cm) of precipitation a year.

Badlands

  • The White River Badlands contain the largest assemblage of known late Eocene and Oligocene mammal fossils.
  • Formations include ammonites, nautiloids, fish, marine reptiles, and turtles.
  • The lighter-colored Sharps Formation was deposited from 28 to 30 million years ago by wind and water as the climate continued to dry and cool.
  • rosion began in the Badlands about 500,000 years ago when the Cheyenne River captured streams and rivers flowing from the Black Hills into the Badlands region.

Carlsbad Caverns

  • In 2003, a park employee found a piece of a stone scraper within view of Carlsbad Cavern's entrance that goes back to Ice Age Indian hunters.
  • In 2004, archeologists found fragments of two spear points of the Midland-style Paleo Indian projectile points of some 10,000 years ago.
  • Carlsbad Caverns National Park is located in the Guadalupe Mountains, a mountain range that runs from west Texas into southeastern New Mexico.
  • Carlsbad Caverns National Park contains more than 119 limestone caves that are outstanding in the profusion, diversity and beauty of their formations.
