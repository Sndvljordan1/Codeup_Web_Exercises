National Parks Trivia!

Which park has the tallest mountains?
Mt. McKinley, the highest point in North America at 20,320 feet, can be found in the Denali National Park and Preserve, AK
How low can we go?
The lowest point, on the surface, in the entire Western Hemisphere is in Death Valley National park, located in California and 282 feet below sea level.
Which Presidents have the most sites named for them?
Abraham Lincoln and Teddy Roosevelt both come out on top with four sites each
What state includes the most National Park Service units?
California leads the way with 23.
Where would you find the "Niagara of the South"?
Cumberland Falls, the largest waterfall east of the Rockies (except Niagara) is known as the "Niagara of the South". Clear water pours over rocks in a fall of more than 60 feet and 125 feet wide. The waterfall is the centerpiece of Cumberland Falls State Park in Kentucky.
Can you name the largest unit of the National Park System?
The Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Preserve in Alaska covers 13.2 million acres, including 8.7 million acres of wilderness area.
Where would you find the deepest lake in the United States?
At 1,932 feet, Crater Lake is the deepest lake in the United States. It is the centerpiece of Crater Lake National Park, which is located in southern Oregon.
Where would you find the longest recorded cave system in the world?
Mammoth Cave National Park in Kentucky includes more than 336 miles of explored and mapped caves.
In what state would you find a park that commemorates the completion of the Transcontinental Railroad?
In Utah. The Golden Spike National Historic Site marks the joining of the Central Pacific and Union Pacific railroads in 1869.
Can you name a park that once served as a World War II internment camp?
Located in California's Owens Valley, Manzanar National Historic Site preserves one of ten camps at which Japanese American citizens and Japanese aliens were interned during World War II.

Parks List

Arches

  • There are over 2,000 cataloged arches in Arches National Park. In order to be considered an arch, an opening must measure at least three feet (in any direction).
  • Arches is located in a "high desert," with elevations ranging from 4,085 to 5,653 feet above sea level.
  • On a daily basis, temperatures may fluctuate as much as 50 degrees.
  • The forces of nature have acted in concert to create the landscape of Arches, which contains the greatest density of natural arches in the world.
  • On average, the park receives 8-10 inches (18-23 cm) of precipitation a year.

Badlands

  • The White River Badlands contain the largest assemblage of known late Eocene and Oligocene mammal fossils.
  • Formations include ammonites, nautiloids, fish, marine reptiles, and turtles.
  • The lighter-colored Sharps Formation was deposited from 28 to 30 million years ago by wind and water as the climate continued to dry and cool.
  • rosion began in the Badlands about 500,000 years ago when the Cheyenne River captured streams and rivers flowing from the Black Hills into the Badlands region.

Carlsbad Caverns

  • In 2003, a park employee found a piece of a stone scraper within view of Carlsbad Cavern's entrance that goes back to Ice Age Indian hunters.
  • In 2004, archeologists found fragments of two spear points of the Midland-style Paleo Indian projectile points of some 10,000 years ago.
  • Carlsbad Caverns National Park is located in the Guadalupe Mountains, a mountain range that runs from west Texas into southeastern New Mexico.
  • Carlsbad Caverns National Park contains more than 119 limestone caves that are outstanding in the profusion, diversity and beauty of their formations.
